Research

Research interests and projects of Weizheng Bai

Research Interests

My research addresses engineering and safety questions in underground energy storage, with a primary focus on salt cavern hydrogen storage.

  • Underground hydrogen storage: feasibility, storage capacity, thermodynamic behavior, and operational performance.
  • Hydrogen seepage and containment: transport in surrounding rock, leakage pathways, well integrity, and risk assessment.
  • Geomechanics of salt caverns: deformation, stability, long-term operation, and the influence of impurities and sediments.
  • Renewable energy integration: coupling wind and solar power, electrolysis, hydrogen storage, and grid peaking.
  • Artificial intelligence: data-driven site screening, geological suitability evaluation, and operational decision support.

Current Project

National Key R&D Program of China

Applicability and Key Safety Technologies of Geological Hydrogen Storage Facilities
December 2024 - November 2027

This project develops methods for evaluating geological sealing and stability. The work integrates geological structure, surrounding-rock properties, chamber geometry, stress-field distribution, formation fluids, and environmental constraints to:

  • establish a geological applicability evaluation index system;
  • evaluate sealing and mechanical stability;
  • identify favorable regions and candidate blocks;
  • support safe design and deployment of geological hydrogen storage.

Completed Project

Key Technologies for Underground Hydrogen Storage

January 2023 - December 2024

The project focused on:

  • hydrogen transport patterns in the surrounding rock of underground storage facilities;
  • a suitability assessment system for geological hydrogen storage;
  • compilation and analysis of geological data for prospective storage formations in China;
  • screening of feasible sites for underground hydrogen storage development.
