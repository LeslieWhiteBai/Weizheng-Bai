Weizheng Bai

PhD Student in Civil Engineering | Underground Hydrogen Storage

Email GitHub ORCID ResearchGate CV

I am a researcher in civil and geotechnical engineering at the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences. My work focuses on the safe, large-scale use of underground geological formations for hydrogen and other forms of energy storage. I combine multiphysics simulation, geomechanics, site evaluation, and risk assessment to study how salt caverns can support renewable energy systems.

Research Focus

Underground Hydrogen Storage

Storage capacity, operating performance, and engineering feasibility of salt caverns and other geological structures.

Hydrogen Seepage & Safety

Hydrogen transport through surrounding rock, well integrity, containment performance, and leakage risk assessment.

Intelligent Energy Storage

Artificial intelligence and data-driven methods for site screening, suitability evaluation, and operational decision-making.

Research Experience

National Key R&D Program of China: Applicability and Key Safety Technologies of Geological Hydrogen Storage Facilities
December 2024 - November 2027

Developing evaluation indices for geological sealing and stability, integrating geological structure, rock properties, chamber geometry, stress fields, formation fluids, and environmental constraints to support favorable-block selection.

Research and Development of Key Technologies for Underground Hydrogen Storage
January 2023 - December 2024

Studied hydrogen transport in surrounding rock, established a geological suitability evaluation system, and screened feasible geological sites for underground hydrogen storage in China.
